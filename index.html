RETROSPECT

Reminisce the retro gaming years and relive your inner child!

FEATURED

Pokemon Red and Blue launched in 1996, tasking players with catching, training, and battling creatures called Pokémon. Created by Satoshi Tajiri, Pokémon became a global phenomenon, with games, TV shows, movies, and merchandise. Pokémon has influenced various aspects of pop culture, including trading card games, toys, and even mobile gaming with Pokémon GO.

Pikachu

Pikachu is a large yellow mouse with a lightning bolt-shaped tail, and red sacs on its cheek which can generate large amounts of electricity. Pikachu was created by Ken Sugimori, one of the original designers of Pokémon. Pikachu has become a global phenomenon and a symbol of Japanese culture. Pikachu is the mascot of the Pokémon franchise, and has appeared in almost every media adaptation of it, such as anime, manga, movies, trading cards, merchandise, and more.

TOP 5 RETRO CHARACTERS

1

Bowser

Bowser, sometimes known as King Koopa or King Bowser, is the main antagonist of the Super Mario franchise. He is a large, powerful, fire-breathing Koopa.

2

Mario

Mario is always bright and cheerful and instantly recognizable with his blue overalls, red cap, and trademark moustache.

3

Charizard

An orange, dragon-like Pokémon, Charizard is the evolved form of Charmeleon and the final evolution of Charmander.

4

Ryu

Ryu is the main protagonist of the Street Fighter series, first appearing in the original Street Fighter.

5

Princess Zelda

Princess Zelda is a character in The Legend of Zelda video games. She often gets captured by Ganon and has to be saved by Link.
